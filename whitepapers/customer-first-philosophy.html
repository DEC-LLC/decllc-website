The Industry's Problem: Vendor Lock-In Disguised as Solutions

Every major infrastructure vendor wants the same thing: to own your entire stack. Buy their firewall, then their management platform, then their cloud, then their analytics. Each product works best — or only — with their other products. Switching costs increase with every purchase. This is by design.

How They Think

  • Customer buys our firewall
  • Now they need our management console
  • Now they need our cloud service
  • Now they need our analytics
  • Now they can't leave

How We Think

  • Customer has a FortiGate. That's fine.
  • We'll monitor it alongside their Cisco switches
  • We'll back up its config alongside their VMs
  • When the FortiGate contract expires, they can choose
  • They stay because it works, not because they're trapped

The industry treats customers as accounts to capture. We treat them as engineers with real problems who deserve tools that work with what they already have.

Making Cloud vs On-Prem Meaningless

The industry wants you to choose: are you a "cloud company" or an "on-prem company"? This is a false choice invented by vendors who profit from the distinction.

The reality is that most organizations have both. They have servers in a rack and instances in AWS. They have VMs on Proxmox and containers on GCP. They need tools that don't care about the distinction.

DEC-LLC products run the same way everywhere:

  • Same image. The QCOW2 that runs on your KVM host converts to an AMI for AWS, a VHD for Azure, or uploads directly to OCI.
  • Same configuration. Firewall rules, VPN tunnels, monitoring targets, backup policies — all defined the same way regardless of where the product runs.
  • Same UI. One management interface, whether you're managing an appliance in your server room or an instance in Frankfurt.
  • Same authority. The product controls its own DNS, DHCP, identity, and security. The cloud provider provides compute. We provide authority.

What This Means for Your Team

Your engineers learn one set of tools. Your policies are defined once. Your audit trail is in one place. When you move a workload from on-prem to cloud — or back — the infrastructure layer moves with it. No re-architecture. No new training. No new vendors.

Vendor Agnostic by Design

Being vendor agnostic is not a marketing claim. It is an engineering decision baked into every product:

  • NIVMIA monitors, manages, and corrects Cisco, Juniper, Arista, Fortinet, and any device that speaks SNMP or has an SSH CLI — including ISP-provided routers, MSP-managed firewalls, and carrier CPE. It standardizes configs across your fleet, detects and corrects VLAN misconfigurations, validates BGP/OSPF routing tables, suppresses broadcast storms, plans bandwidth and QoS capacity, and implements changes. If it's on your network, NIVMIA controls it.
  • IVMIA manages VMs across every hypervisor and cloud, plus the full endpoint fleet — Windows desktops, MacBooks, Linux workstations, POS terminals, field devices. It handles resource allocation and redistribution, hardware compatibility checks, workload placement, and capacity planning across the entire compute estate.
  • OpenUTM secures the entire perimeter — on-premises, cloud, and hybrid. Same image, same policies, same UI whether it's an appliance in your rack or an instance in AWS.
  • VaultSync backs up everything — VMs, physical servers, network configs, workstations, and company mobile devices. It extracts business data from backups: contact lists become company directories, config history becomes compliance evidence.

The customer's existing investment is respected. We integrate with what works. We offer to replace what doesn't. The customer decides, not us.

Open Source Community Tier

Every DEC-LLC product has a free, open-source community tier. This is not a trial. It is not feature-limited to the point of uselessness. It is a fully functional product that a small business or home lab can use indefinitely without paying anything.

Why? Because trust is earned, not purchased. We want engineers to use our software, form opinions, and make recommendations based on experience. If the community tier solves the problem, use it. If you need more — enterprise features, professional support, SLA guarantees — the commercial tiers are there.

This model has a direct business benefit: every community user is a potential advocate. Every lab deployment is a proof of concept. The software sells itself because it works, not because a salesperson called.

What We Believe

  1. The customer's infrastructure is theirs, not ours. We provide tools to manage it. We do not hold it hostage.
  2. Interoperability is not optional. If we can't work with what the customer already has, we haven't built the right product.
  3. Cloud and on-prem are the same problem. The answer should be the same tools, the same policies, the same experience.
  4. Trust is earned by working, not by promising. Ship software that works. Open-source the community tier. Let engineers decide.
  5. Integration beats displacement. Helping a customer use their FortiGate better is more valuable than convincing them to throw it away.
  6. The platform should disappear. If the customer has to think about our platform instead of their problems, we've failed. The best infrastructure software is invisible.

We don't sell lock-in. We sell freedom — the freedom to run any vendor, any cloud, any hypervisor, and know that your tools work the same way everywhere. The customer stays because it works, not because they can't leave.
