What it is, at a high level

DEC QDX is DEC-LLC's post-quantum, multi-channel secure transport. It is built around a small number of unifying design properties: post-quantum native (no retrofit), multi-channel by construction (a single session can deliver across heterogeneous media concurrently), threshold-resilient (the session survives partial channel loss by design), and deployment-agnostic at the implementation layer.

QDX is the subject of a U.S. provisional patent application filed in 2026 by Diwan Enterprise Consulting LLC. The provisional was prepared by a USPTO-registered patent agent and a non-provisional conversion is in planning.

How DEC-LLC monetizes QDX

QDX is offered as licensable intellectual property, not as a packaged appliance for general sale. Strategic licensees include:

  • Defense primes and integrator-of-record contractors operating in multi-medium environments
  • Satellite operators and ground-segment providers with concurrent RF, optical, and terrestrial paths
  • Federal and federated-trust customers under the NIST post-quantum migration mandate
  • Hardware partners interested in FPGA bitstream, ASIC RTL, or IP-core implementations
  • Software platform vendors integrating PQC-native transport into their own product lines

Deployment forms available for licensing include software libraries, firmware images, FPGA bitstream IP, ASIC RTL/IP-core, and software-only embedded integrations. The specific commercial form is negotiated per licensee.

Why technical materials are NDA-gated

QDX is mid-prosecution and competitively sensitive. The architecture is replicable by capable teams given enough detail, and DEC-LLC's strategic posture is to control depth-of-disclosure rather than publish openly while the patent estate is still being built out. Technical reading materials (whitepaper, reader's guide, integration spec, performance benchmarks) are made available to qualified counterparties after a Non-Disclosure Agreement is in place.

The NDA process is light: a one-way confidentiality agreement, electronic-signature acceptable, no special legal review required on your side for ordinary investor or commercial-evaluation use.
