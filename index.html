The problem isn't your team or your tech. It's the gaps between your tools.

Most IT trouble doesn't start with a hacker. It starts in the space between systems that were never built to talk to each other. A firewall change nobody told the network team about. A backup that silently stopped working because a credential rotated. A switch that's been reporting problems — on a dashboard that nobody opens because it's the fifth dashboard in the stack.

Your team isn't missing things because they're careless. They're missing things because they're asked to watch a dozen tools that don't share a single conversation, keep a single log, or agree on a single version of the truth.

Think of our appliances as a building's quiet night crew — the ones who walk every floor, test every alarm, rotate every key, and check every valve while everyone else sleeps. By the time the morning shift arrives, the problems from last night are already solved, documented, and in the logbook.

We build five appliances that do exactly this — for your network, your servers, your firewall, your backups, and your ability to move between clouds without asking permission. They run on your hardware, under your authority, with no phone-home required, and they keep doing their job when the threats on the other side of the wire get stranger than they are today.

IVMIA

Every computer you own, on one screen.

Your virtualization bill doubled. Your clusters were set up before the team expanded. Half your VMs might be idle — but nobody can tell you which half. IVMIA sees every virtual machine, every hypervisor, every cloud instance — from one dashboard. It shows you who's using what, which VMs are orphaned, which are critical, and gives you the power to move workloads between platforms — telling you how to save money on your clouds and on-prem or hosted systems, and safeguard those systems from CPU, memory, and disk bottlenecks without incurring fees or even downtime.

NIVMIA

Your network, from a single seat.

You have 40 switches from 6 vendors. When someone says "the wifi is slow in Building 3," you open four dashboards to find out why. NIVMIA logs into every device, every night, and tells you what changed, what's drifting, and what's about to fail — in plain English. "Port 14 on 10GSwitch-2 has been dropping 8% of packets for three days." New staff and interns can ask the system why something is configured the way it is and get a clear answer — so your senior people don't have to stop what they're doing to explain it.

OpenUTM

A firewall that knows why its rules exist — and can teach your new hire.

Every firewall accumulates rules that nobody remembers the context for. Your lead engineer just got promoted — or is heads-down on the new acquisition. The junior analyst needs to take over. OpenUTM is intelligent: ask it "why is port 8443 open to 10.5.0.0/24?" and it tells you — the context, the ticket, the reason. When new staff arrive, the system itself becomes the onboarding. Company knowledge stops being bottlenecked in one person and becomes something the whole team can access, permanently.

VaultSync

Backups you can actually restore — to any platform.

You have backups. Probably. Somewhere. When was the last time anyone tested a restore? Would they survive a ransomware attack that targets the backup server too? VaultSync backs up your VMs, servers, workstations, network configs, and company phones — encrypted, tested, and restorable to a completely different platform if the one you're on goes dark. If any vendor changes their terms tomorrow, you restore to a different platform and keep going.

SDNS

When your products work as one.

SDNS — Software Defined Systems Networking and Storage — is not a product you buy. It's what happens when two or more DEC-LLC products are connected: a shared databus, cross-product AI agents, and event federation that turn separate tools into a single coordinated infrastructure. Add a user once and they exist everywhere. Rotate a key once and every product inherits it. The coordination layer isn't something you install — it emerges when the suite talks to each other.

IPv6Auto

Move to the new internet without breaking the old one.

The old internet is running out of addresses. The new one (IPv6) is already required by US federal agencies and rolling toward everyone else. Most organizations haven't started. IPv6Auto migrates your network without downtime, rewrites your firewall rules for the new addressing, verifies everything works, and leaves you compliant — while the old addresses keep working until you're ready to let them go.

FireMigrate

Replace your firewall without rewriting your rules.

Your firewall vendor got acquired, raised prices, or reached end-of-life. The replacement means translating thousands of rules by hand — which means risk, which means nobody wants to do it, which means you're stuck. FireMigrate reads your existing configuration, translates it to the new platform's language, verifies the translation, and hands you a working result. The migration that used to take months takes days.

About DEC-LLC

DEC-LLC (Diwan Enterprise Consulting) builds infrastructure software for organizations that want to own what they deploy. We ship appliances — not cloud services — because we believe a business should not have to ask permission from a vendor to keep its own systems running.
